## Company Brief

**What Linear is:** Project management and issue tracking for software teams. Linear is opinionated in a way most project management tools aren't — it's fast (keyboard-first, near-instant sync), has a clear design philosophy (fewer features done better), and has deliberately avoided the feature bloat that defines Jira, Asana, and most of the competition. It's built for teams that care about how their tools feel, not just what they do.

**Business model:** SaaS, primarily PLG — teams adopt bottom-up, then organizations standardize on it. No sales team for most of their growth. Pricing is per-seat, with a team tier and an enterprise tier. They've been expanding enterprise offerings as customers scale.

**Scale and trajectory:** Estimated $35M ARR (2025, reported in industry sources — not confirmed publicly). Series C, raised ~$35M from Index Ventures, Sequoia, and others at a $400M+ valuation. Profitable or near-profitable based on their stated operating philosophy. Headcount is small — estimated 50-70 employees, intentionally lean. Karri Saarinen is the founder and CEO; he was previously a design lead at Coinbase. Jori Lallo (CTO) and Tuomas Artman (founding engineer) round out the founding team.

**Product trajectory:** Linear has been expanding from core issue tracking into broader project management (Cycles for sprint management, Projects for longer-horizon planning). The integrations layer (GitHub, GitLab, Figma, Slack) is a meaningful investment. AI features are on the roadmap — automated status updates, blocker surfacing, delivery prediction — and this role likely sits at the intersection of those.

**Recent signals:** No major negative news. Linear is one of the few developer tools companies that has maintained its brand identity as it's scaled. Strong NPS in developer tooling surveys. The product community talks about Linear the way they talked about Notion in 2019 — as a rare case of a tool that gets better rather than more complicated as it grows.

**Health signals:** Profitable philosophy, lean team, strong brand. No public layoffs. Series C suggests they have runway and are not in distress. The risk is scale — Linear has strong product-market fit at the team level and is still proving the enterprise motion.

## Why This Job

**Honest internal answer:** Linear is one of the few developer tools companies that I'd actually be excited to join for the craft, not just the brand. The product has a genuine design philosophy — something you can feel using it — and the challenge of making project management work for engineering organizations at scale is one I've thought about from the Notion side. It's not AI-first in the way Anthropic or Glean is, but the developer experience PM scope here is broader and more interesting than the average PM role at a bigger company. Also: SF, hybrid, comp range clears my floor, and Linear's brand is the right signal for where I want to be in 3 years.

**What to say in the interview:**

"I've been following Linear since the beta and I've used it personally. What drew me in wasn't just the speed — it was that Linear seems to actually have opinions about how project management should work, rather than trying to be everything to everyone. I've spent the last few years at Notion thinking about how complex tools scale to large organizations — workspace governance, admin controls, how you make something that's elegant for a 5-person team not fall apart at 500 users. That's exactly the challenge I'd be stepping into here, and it's the kind of product problem I find genuinely interesting. I'm looking for a role where I can own a problem space with real stakes, and this looks like that."

## How to Position Yourself

**Lead story: Notion workspace governance redesign.** This maps most directly to what Linear needs from a developer experience PM who's thinking about enterprise. The story: found that activation was blocked by a governance problem (not a product quality problem), rebuilt the permissions model, shipped admin tooling, moved NPS +12 and time-to-activation -40%. Demonstrates: diagnostic skill, system thinking, enterprise empathy, and the ability to work on the unsexy-but-critical layer.

**Second story: Intercom account owner persona.** Shows the diagnostic pattern — running experiments, rejecting the inherited roadmap, going upstream to find the real problem. Linear would want a PM who can question the obvious solutions. The Intercom story is the best evidence of that.

**Third story (if they ask about developer focus): FreelanceOS.** Founder credibility. Built the full product. Did the thing I'd be building for. That authenticity matters for a tool used by engineers — they can tell when a PM has never shipped code or managed a backlog.

**Specific bullets to anchor on:**
- Rebuilt Notion's permissions model for 500+ user organizations — the kind of systems thinking Linear will need as they go enterprise
- Admin AI feature gating framework — shows I understand the compliance layer that enterprise customers require (relevant as Linear adds AI features)
- Intercom prototype: validated hypothesis with a 2-week prototype across 40K accounts before funding the full build — shows I know when to experiment vs. build

**What not to lead with:** The PLG-to-enterprise conversion motion at Notion. Linear already has the bottoms-up adoption pattern figured out. They need someone who can make the product work at scale, not someone who can find new enterprise revenue. Save that for if they ask about enterprise growth.

**On AI:** Linear's AI work is early. Don't make AI the centerpiece of the pitch — that could read as not caring about the core product craft. But if they ask about AI, the Notion AI framework story is the right one: not "I built AI features" but "I built the infrastructure that made AI safe to deploy in enterprise environments."

## Questions to Ask

**On the role:**
1. "You described this as evolving from a great issue tracker to the system of record for how engineering teams understand their work. What does success look like for that shift in 12 months — what's the product you want to have shipped?"
2. "Why is this role open? Is this a new PM headcount or are you backfilling someone?"
3. "How does the PM team work with the engineering team — are PMs embedded in specific engineering squads, or is the team organized differently?"

**On the product:**
4. "Where is the biggest gap between what Linear does today and what your enterprise customers need? Is it in the core issue tracking workflows or somewhere else?"
5. "The AI features you mentioned — automated status updates, blocker surfacing — where are those on the roadmap, and how mature is the thinking there?"

**On culture:**
6. "Linear is known for a strong product philosophy and a small team. How does that show up in how PM decisions get made — is it consensus-driven, is there a strong top-down product vision, something else?"
7. "How does the team handle disagreement about product direction, especially between PMs and engineering leadership?"

**If the conversation goes long:**
8. "What's been the hardest product decision Linear has made in the last 18 months, and how did it get made?"

## Comp and Logistics to Confirm

**Comp range:** JD says $190K–$260K base + equity. I need to understand the equity component — what's the strike price, what's the current 409A, what's the last round valuation and the cap table dilution picture. This matters a lot given the $400M+ valuation and the question of whether there's meaningful upside left.

**Floor:** Base needs to be $200K+ for an established company. Total comp (base + equity fair value) needs to clear $300K. This role can clear that if the equity is real — but I need to pressure test the equity math.

**What to probe:**
- "The JD gives a range of $190K–$260K — where in that range does this role sit, and how does that depend on experience level?"
- "Can you share the equity package structure — number of options, vesting schedule, and the current 409A valuation? I want to make sure I can do the math."
- "How many interview stages are remaining after this call, and what's your typical timeline from first round to offer?"
- "Is this role hybrid — 3 days in office as the JD mentions — or has that changed?"
