# Job Description — Linear | Sr. PM, Developer Experience

**Source:** linear.app/careers

---

Linear is a project management tool built for software teams. We believe the tools engineers use to manage their work should be as thoughtfully designed as the software they build. We're used by thousands of engineering teams who depend on Linear every day to plan, track, and ship.

We're looking for a Senior Product Manager to own developer experience across Linear's core product surfaces.

**Location:** San Francisco, CA or New York, NY (hybrid, 3 days/week)
**Compensation:** $190,000 – $260,000 base + equity

---

## About the role

This is not a feature maintenance role. We are at a stage where the product needs to evolve from a great issue tracker to the system of record for how engineering teams understand and manage their work. The person in this role will help define what that means and own the work to get there.

You'll work on:

**Core project management workflows.** Issues, Cycles, Projects — the surfaces that engineering managers, tech leads, and CTOs use to plan work, track dependencies, and understand delivery across teams. These are the surfaces Linear is known for, and they need to get substantially better as our customers scale.

**The integrations layer.** Linear connects to GitHub, GitLab, Figma, Slack, and dozens of third-party tools. We want every developer's workflow to run through or connect to Linear — both inbound (code activity syncing to issues) and outbound (Linear status surfacing in the places teams already work). The integrations PM owns the strategy and roadmap for this surface.

**The Linear API and developer platform.** We have a public API that thousands of developers use to build internal tooling, automation, and integrations. This is a real developer platform with real developers building on it. The PM who owns this surface needs to think about API ergonomics, documentation quality, and the experience of building on top of Linear.

**AI-assisted project management.** We're building AI features into the core workflow: automated status updates, intelligent blocker surfacing, velocity analysis, delivery prediction. This is early but important work — we want AI to make the management layer disappear, not make it louder.

**Engineering leadership experience.** Engineering managers and CTOs are important users of Linear. They need to understand the state of work across many teams without being in the weeds of every issue. Making that experience compelling is a significant product challenge.

---

## What we're looking for

- 6+ years of product experience, with meaningful time on developer tools, technical infrastructure, or products used daily by engineering teams
- Deep intuition for what engineers value: speed, control, clarity, and the absence of friction
- Experience shipping products with API and integration surface area
- Comfort operating at the product strategy level — this role requires defining the problems, not just solving stated ones
- Exceptional writing. Linear is a highly async culture. Written communication is how decisions get made.
- A portfolio of shipped work you're proud of, with evidence of your reasoning process

**Bonus:**
- You've been an engineering manager, tech lead, or ran sprints — you've done the thing you're building for
- Engineering background that gives you authentic credibility with engineering teams
- Experience thinking about AI-assisted workflows at a product level

## Why Linear

We're small, profitable, and we make decisions accordingly. We're not optimizing for growth at any cost. We have strong opinions about how software should be built and we make products that reflect those opinions. If you want to work on a product where every detail matters and where your work is used by tens of thousands of engineering teams every day, this is that role.

Compensation: $190,000–$260,000 base, equity, full benefits, $3,000 home office stipend.
