## Interview Prep

### Company Context (Relevant to Alex's Priorities)

**Linear's actual challenge right now:** They've nailed product-market fit at the team level (small and mid-size engineering teams). The challenge is scaling that to larger organizations — not just adding seats, but making the product work for organizations with 50+ engineers, multiple teams, cross-team dependency management, and IT admins who need to govern the deployment. This is exactly the Notion enterprise problem in a new context.

**The AI opportunity at Linear:** They're early on AI features. The products they've described (automated status updates, blocker surfacing, delivery prediction) are exactly the kind of AI that could reduce the cognitive load of engineering management without adding noise. The risk: AI features that generate false positives or noise are worse than no AI features for engineering teams, who have zero tolerance for tools that cry wolf.

**What makes Linear's culture unusual:** Karri Saarinen has been public about Linear's philosophy — fewer, better features; speed as a core product value; no feature bloat. This is a company where "no" to a feature is respected. For a PM who prefers that culture, it's a strong signal. For a PM who wants to ship a lot of features quickly, it might be frustrating.

### Talking Points Mapped to JD

**"Core project management workflows"**
- Notion workspace governance redesign: rebuilt the admin layer for 500+ user orgs. The parallel: Linear's Issues/Cycles/Projects need to work cleanly for engineering managers who are managing across 5-10 teams. The PM challenge is the same — making complexity manageable without adding complexity to the product.
- What I learned: the blocker to adoption isn't usually the core workflow — it's the governance and admin layer that IT has to be comfortable with. Linear likely faces this as they go enterprise.

**"Integrations ecosystem"**
- No direct integration PM experience. Be honest: "I don't have a deep background in integration PM work, but I do have experience thinking about how enterprise tools fit into larger workflows — at Notion, one of the core enterprise challenges was making sure Notion played well with the tools companies already used (Slack, email, identity providers). The integration philosophy is the same even if the specific surfaces are different."
- Strong angle: the Linear API and developer platform is a context where my lack of traditional dev tools PM experience is less of a gap — the skills are API design philosophy, developer experience, documentation quality. Those are transferable.

**"AI-assisted project management"**
- Notion AI framework: not "I built AI features" but "I built the infrastructure that made AI safe and trustworthy in enterprise environments." That's the relevant story for Linear's AI work — they'll need admin controls, audit trails, and ways to explain AI-generated insights to skeptical engineers.
- Strong framing: "I'm not a PM who thinks AI should do more — I'm a PM who thinks about when AI helps and when it gets in the way. For engineering teams, the bar for AI intervention is high. I've thought a lot about the trust layer — how you build AI features that engineers actually rely on rather than override."

**"Engineering leadership experience"**
- The Notion PLG-to-enterprise work involved a lot of eng manager and IT admin research. Those users care about different things than end users — they need visibility, control, and accountability. Linear's eng manager experience challenge is the same.
- Question to probe: "Who are the highest-value users for engineering leadership features today — is it the EM, the CTO, or the tech lead? And what's the job-to-be-done that Linear can realistically serve for each of them?"

### Questions to Ask (HM Call)

1. "You described the role as evolving Linear from a great issue tracker to the system of record for how engineering teams understand their work. Where does the product fall short of that today — what's the biggest gap?"
2. "How do you think about the difference between what an engineering manager needs from Linear vs. what an end-user engineer needs? Do those ever come into conflict?"
3. "What's the PM team structure — how many PMs, how are they organized relative to the product surface areas, and how does decision-making work?"
4. "The AI features you're building — where are they on the roadmap in terms of your investment? Is this 'exploring' or 'committed product bet'?"
5. "Why is this role open? Are you adding PM headcount or is this backfill?"
6. "Linear has been known for shipping fewer, better features. How does that play out in practice when there's pressure to add things customers are asking for?"

### How to Close

End the HM call with something like: "I've been genuinely excited about Linear since I started researching this role. The governance and admin challenges you'll face as you scale are ones I've thought about deeply at Notion, and I think there's a real opportunity here to bring that craft to developer tooling specifically. I'd love to understand what the next steps look like."
