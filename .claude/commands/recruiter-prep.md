# Recruiter Prep Skill

Generate a recruiter prep doc for a scheduled recruiter screen.

## The operative principle

A recruiter screen is 30 minutes. The candidate needs to walk in knowing what to say about themselves, why this role, what to ask, and what to probe on comp and logistics. Every section earns its place against that test — nothing else.

## How to reason

Start from the job posting, not from the candidate's history. Read `jd.md` and ask: what does this role actually need? What kind of PM would excel here? What business problem is this company trying to solve?

Then read `me.md` and all files in `references/` — raw source material. Do live web research on the company: recent news, product direction, business model, funding or growth trajectory. Let the evidence determine what leads. Don't produce a generic company overview — surface what's relevant to *this role* and *this candidate's priorities*.

## Sections to produce

**Company brief** — What they do, business model, recent trajectory, anything that signals health or risk. From web research. Not a Wikipedia summary — focus on what a PM candidate should know walking into this call. Go as deep as the evidence warrants.

**Why this job** — Two parts:
- *Honest internal answer:* The real reason, candid, including if it's partly a hedge or fallback.
- *What to say in the interview:* A genuine, interviewable answer — 2-4 sentences, quote format. Should hold up under follow-up.

**How to position yourself** — Which parts of the background lead for this role, which specific projects to name, how to frame the story. Pull from `references/` and, if it exists, `resume.md` in this job's folder — the tailored bullets there already reflect the angle chosen for this role. Be specific: name the project, name the metric, name the mechanism. Don't summarize the career generically.

**Questions to ask** — Tailored to what the user cares about (per `me.md`) and what this role suggests. Not a static bank. Probe: team structure, roadmap, what success looks like in 6 months, why the role is open.

**Comp and logistics to confirm** — What to probe: base/total comp range, remote/hybrid expectations, timeline, number of interview stages remaining.

## Integrity rules

- Never fabricate company facts — use web research and flag uncertainty if sources are thin
- Never fabricate the user's experience — every positioning claim must be backed by `references/`
- Keep metrics exactly as they appear in source material

## Formatting rules

- Save as `recruiter-prep.md` in the job folder
- No em dashes
- No LLM clichés or filler phrasing
- Sections in the order above

## When done

One short paragraph: what angle you led with for positioning and why, anything about the role or company that felt uncertain or worth flagging.
