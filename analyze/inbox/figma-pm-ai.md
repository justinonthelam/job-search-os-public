# Figma — Product Manager, AI

**Location:** San Francisco, CA (hybrid)
**Compensation:** $180,000 – $250,000 base + equity
**Team:** Product — AI

## About Figma

Figma is the collaborative design platform used by over 4 million people to build products, prototypes, and design systems. Design is no longer a solo discipline — it happens in real time, across distributed teams, and at the intersection of design and engineering. Figma is where that work lives.

We're making a significant bet on AI as a fundamental shift in how design work gets done. We're not talking about adding a magic wand button. We're talking about changing the nature of the design process itself.

## About this role

We're looking for a PM to help define and ship the next generation of AI features in Figma. This role sits at the intersection of design workflow, AI capabilities, and the messy realities of how design actually happens in large organizations. You'll work closely with our AI research team, our design systems team, and our enterprise customers.

## What you'll work on

- AI-assisted design generation and iteration — helping designers move from idea to high-fidelity concept faster without compromising their creative control
- Intelligent design system enforcement — using AI to help teams maintain consistency across large design libraries without manual policing
- AI-powered design-to-code workflows — bridging the gap between what designers create and what engineers need to implement it
- Personalization and suggestions within the Figma editor — context-aware recommendations that learn from how individual designers and teams work
- Enterprise AI features — giving IT admins and design leaders the controls and visibility they need to deploy AI responsibly at scale

## What we're looking for

- 5+ years of PM experience, with significant time on consumer or prosumer products where the user's craft is central
- Strong intuition for AI product design — specifically around trust, control, and the moments where AI assistance becomes AI interference
- Experience with PLG-style products where the end user is also an advocate and where word-of-mouth drives adoption
- Track record of shipping AI features that users actually trust and return to, not just features that demo well
- Deep empathy for creative professionals and how they think about their tools — you should understand why a designer would feel threatened by AI and how you design around that
- Strong cross-functional skills — this role requires tight collaboration with AI research, engineering, design, and enterprise sales

## Bonus

- Background in design or design tooling — you've done the work you're building for
- Experience with enterprise AI compliance and rollout — understanding how IT admins think about deploying AI tools in large organizations
- Experience with design systems or component libraries at scale

## Why Figma

Design is becoming a more technical discipline at the same time that engineering is becoming more design-aware. Figma sits at that intersection, and AI is the force that's accelerating both trends. This is a role where the AI work matters — it's not a feature being added to justify a valuation, it's a genuine bet on how collaborative creative work will change.
