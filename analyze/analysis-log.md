## Job Details

### Rippling — Sr. PM, Workforce AI
**Score: 8/10 | 2026-02-08**

- Strong fit on multiple dimensions: enterprise SaaS, admin tooling, and the AI-in-workflow problem all map directly to Notion and Intercom work. The HR SaaS context is new but the underlying patterns — admin as buyer, end user as victim of bad UX, AI as the thing that changes what's possible — are exactly the same.
- Credible candidacy: Rippling is looking for someone who has shipped enterprise features under compliance constraints, understands admin-facing UX, and has built with AI. The Notion AI framework story is directly relevant. Recruiter read should be positive.
- Company signal is strong: $1.2B ARR estimated (2025), raised at $13.5B valuation, IPO widely expected within 12-18 months. Parker Conrad is a known quantity — demanding but respected. This is a real company with real revenue.
- Comp likely clears the floor: Rippling typically pays at or above market for senior PMs. RSU value at IPO trajectory makes the total comp story compelling if the timing is right.
- One flag: Rippling is known for a high-intensity culture and a strong founder personality. Not a dealbreaker, but worth probing in the recruiter screen — what does "Workforce AI" actually mean here, and who owns the AI product strategy.

### Loom — Sr. PM, Enterprise
**Score: 7/10 | 2026-02-08**

- Good fit on the enterprise angle: Loom has been pushing into enterprise async communication workflows, and the problems (admin controls, IT governance, user adoption vs. buyer adoption) map directly to Notion enterprise work. The workspace governance and activation experience is a natural parallel.
- Atlassian acquisition (2023) changes the risk profile: this is no longer a standalone startup bet. It's a product within a larger org, which means more stability but potentially less PM autonomy. Worth understanding how much independence the Loom PM team has post-acquisition.
- AI angle is real but secondary: Loom has AI transcription and summarization features, but the core product is still async video. Not AI-first in the sense I want. Scores here because the enterprise PM craft is relevant, not because the AI work is compelling.
- Brand is B-tier at best: Loom is well-known in certain product circles but not a universal PM prestige signal. Atlassian adds credibility but also bureaucracy. Being a Senior PM at Atlassian/Loom is a fine outcome, not an exciting one.
- Comp likely fine: Atlassian pays competitively, RSU value is clear (public company). Should clear the $300K+ total comp bar.

- Company trajectory is real: Brex hit $500M ARR in 2024, refocused on enterprise after pulling back from SMB. The bet is paying off. Not a company in decline.
- Scores 6 because the craft fits and the brand is fine — but doesn't clear the AI-first bar. Would consider if pipeline dries up or if the role turns out to have more AI depth than the JD suggests.

### Airtable — Sr. PM, Enterprise Platform
**Score: 5/10 | 2026-02-08**

- Skills match is obvious on paper: enterprise platform PM at a collaborative tool with a complex permissions model. The Notion overlap is real. But "obvious fit on paper" is not the same as "this is a good move."
- Trajectory is the problem: Airtable raised at an $11B valuation in 2021, laid off 27% of staff in 2023, and has been trying to reframe itself as an AI platform without a clear product bet that's working. The "enterprise platform" role looks like consolidation work, not growth work.
- AI direction feels defensive: Airtable is adding AI features to avoid being disrupted, not because AI is central to what the product does. That's the exact situation I left Notion to avoid — building on top of an existing tool rather than building something where AI is the core mechanism.
- Brand has diluted: Airtable was a stronger signal 3-4 years ago. In 2026, it reads as a mature no-code tool with uncertain trajectory. Not a bad brand but not one that opens new doors.
- Would take this if the scope were genuinely expansive and the role had real ownership over AI product direction. As written, it doesn't.
